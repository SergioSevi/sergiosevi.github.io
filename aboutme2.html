About me

I am a researcher studying the imprints of extensions of the standard model into the cosmological history of the universe. You can find my academic history and a brief description of my research interests here. You can find my full CV here

Academic history

My academic journey began with a Master's degree in Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics at the University of Cambridge, where I developed a strong foundation in complex physical theories. This experience inspired me to pursue a PhD in Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Nottingham, focusing on scalar-tensor theories of gravity and their mathematical and physical implications.

After earning my PhD, I joined Durham University as a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Physics and the Institute for Particle Physics Phenomenology. Here, I collaborate with leading experts in high-energy physics and cosmology, working on developing new theoretical models and computational tools. This role allows me to merge my passion for physics with a commitment to advancing scientific knowledge.

Research interests

During my PhD at the University of Nottingham, I focused on scalar-tensor theories of gravity, producing significant research on various cosmological phenomena. I authored three key papers: one on fifth forces, exploring their potential observational signatures and implications for fundamental physics; another on the development of FeynMG, a Mathematica code extending FeynRules to handle gravitational interactions in scalar-tensor theories; and a third paper investigating early dark energy models (EDOs) as a possible resolution to the Hubble tension.


At Durham University, as a postdoctoral researcher, I have continued to advance my research in high-energy physics and cosmology. My work here includes a notable paper that tests early dark energy models on the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB), providing crucial insights into how these models could explain observed cosmological discrepancies. This research contributes to a deeper understanding of the universe's expansion history and the fundamental forces at play.
